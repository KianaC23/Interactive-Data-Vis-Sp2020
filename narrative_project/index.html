Diversity in the Director's Chair

For this project I initially wanted to look at diversity among directors of the most popular films of the last ten years. Most articles pointed to the same study by the Annenberg Foundation at USC that puts out an annual report on diversity among the major film studios. They touch on many factors surrounding the disparity between women, undersrepesented groups and their white male counterparts. This includes hiring practices, which genre directors get to film and much more. The study started in 2007 so I made a dataset using the top 100 grossing films (domestic) from 2007 -2019 using their study as a touchstone. My numbers are not exactly the same as they publish before the final totals for each year are reported. Overall Hollywood has alot of work to do. For my project I focused on women, specifically women of color. In the 1300 movies I analyzed only 12 of them were directed by women of color. There were only 10 individuals who directed the films.
